The Core of Brightum

Uniquely – retrieved Marine Polysaccharides

Polysaccharides are the long-chain molecules of carbohydrates. They are one of the most essential biomolecules for our life. Marine polysaccharides, in particular, are some of the most abundant sources for bioactive substances. Today, the health benefits of marine polysaccharides have been solidly demonstrated from laboratory-scale experiments to clinical studies.

The bioactive properties of seaweed – derived polysaccharides significantly depend on the source of the seaweed and how they are extracted and processed. Thanks to the specially – sourced seaweeds and uniquely-created extraction processes, 3 potent bioactive are stably retrieved at maximum potency. This is the core of Brightum.

Hydrolytic oligo-saccharides from Atlantic Ocean Gigartina seaweed.

HMW fucoidan complexes from Atlantic Ocean Ascophyllum seaweed.

Highly sulfated oligo-fucoidans from Iroise Sea Laminaria (kelp) seaweed.

The Precise of Brightum

It is the precise molecules that makes Brightum bioactive

Bioactive properties of marine polysaccharides are highly dependent on their source of origin, purity, size, compositional traits and the content of sulfate. By using specifically – sourced seaweeds, plus uniquely – created extraction and processing, highly-purified marine polysaccharides with the right size, compositional trait and content of sulfate are obtained. These are precise molecules of marine polysaccharides that demonstrate superior age – defying effects.

The Precise Sulfate Content

Highly sulfated oligo-fucoidans are extremely small polysaccharide molecules composed of only one, two or three highly-sulfated saccharide units. Unlike other saccharide unit, these saccharide units are fully saturated with sulfates. Sulfate plays a pivotal role in the display of bioactivities.

1.

Fucoidans extracted from Laminaria seaweed of the Iroise Sea are especially rich in bioactive sulfate content.

2.

A unique hydrolytic process is used to precisely retrieve ultimate bioactive small molecules. These are mono, di- or trimers of highly sulfated Laminaria fucoidans.
